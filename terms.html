1. Program Name

These Terms & Conditions (“Terms”) govern the Radiology Order Conversion Engine (ROCE) Patient Scheduling SMS Program (“Program,” “Service,” “we,” or “us”). The Program is operated by ROCE on behalf of participating radiology practices.

2. Program Description

The Program sends transactional, scheduling-related text messages to patients of participating radiology practices in connection with already-ordered imaging exams (including MRI, CT, ultrasound, and DEXA). Messages may include:

  • An invitation to schedule an ordered imaging exam, with a secure expiring link.
  • Confirmation requests for an upcoming appointment.
  • Reminders before a scheduled appointment (typically at 72 hours, 24 hours, and 3 hours).
  • Replies to HELP and STOP keywords.
  • Customer-care responses to inbound questions or callback requests.

The Program is not used for marketing, promotional offers, third-party advertising, or any communication unrelated to scheduling and completing the patient’s ordered imaging exam.

3. How Patients Opt In (Consent)

Patients are enrolled in the Program only after providing consent to be contacted for scheduling purposes by the radiology practice or its authorized vendor. Consent is collected through a patient intake form before SMS messages are sent.

  • The patient provides or confirms their mobile phone number.
  • The patient checks an unchecked SMS consent box that says: I agree to receive appointment reminders via SMS. Msg & data rates may apply. Reply STOP to opt out.
  • The intake form explains the expected message types, message frequency, message-and-data-rates disclosure, HELP/STOP instructions, and links to these Terms and the Privacy Policy.
  • The consent record is stored with patient ID, mobile phone number, consent timestamp, consent source, and Terms/Privacy version.

By opting in, the patient agrees to receive recurring automated text messages from the Program in connection with their imaging order(s). Consent is not a condition of receiving medical care.

4. Message Frequency

Message frequency varies by patient. A typical patient receives between 1 and 6 messages per imaging order: an initial scheduling invitation, optional follow-ups if no response, appointment reminders, and a confirmation request. Patients with multiple imaging orders may receive more.

5. Message and Data Rates

Message and data rates may apply. The Program does not charge patients to participate, but the patient’s mobile carrier may charge for sending or receiving SMS or MMS messages depending on the patient’s plan. Patients should consult their wireless carrier for details.

6. Supported Carriers

The Program is intended to support all major U.S. wireless carriers, including (without limitation) AT&T, Verizon, T-Mobile, US Cellular, Sprint, Boost, Cricket, MetroPCS, and Google Fi. Carriers are not liable for delayed or undelivered messages.

7. Help Instructions

For help at any time, reply HELP to any Program message. Patients will receive an automated reply with a brief description of the Program and customer-care contact information. Patients can also contact us directly using the information in Section 11 below.

8. Opt-Out Instructions

Patients can stop receiving Program messages at any time by replying STOP (or END, CANCEL, UNSUBSCRIBE, or QUIT) to any Program message. After opting out, the patient will receive a single confirmation message and no further Program messages will be sent. To re-enroll, the patient may text START to the Program number or contact the practice to be re-enrolled by staff.

Opting out of SMS does not prevent the practice from contacting the patient through other appropriate channels (such as phone calls or mailed notices) about their imaging order, when permitted by law.

9. Eligibility

The Program is intended for patients of participating radiology practices in the United States. Patients must be the authorized user of the mobile number provided. By participating, the patient confirms that they are at least the age of majority in their jurisdiction or that a parent or legal guardian is providing consent on their behalf.
