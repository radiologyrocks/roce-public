Privacy Policy

ROCE Patient Scheduling SMS Program

← Back to home

Effective date: April 30, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how the Radiology Order Conversion Engine (ROCE) (“ROCE,” “Program,” “we,” or “us”) collects, uses, protects, and shares information in connection with the patient scheduling SMS program operated on behalf of participating radiology practices.

Plain-English summary: We only collect what we need to help schedule your imaging exam. We do not sell your data. We do not share your data with third parties for their own marketing or advertising. We do not use your data for marketing purposes.

1. Information We Collect

In the course of operating the SMS scheduling program, we may collect and process the following categories of information:

  • Identifiers and contact information — first name, mobile phone number, and an internal patient identifier provided by the radiology practice.
  • Imaging order metadata — the type of imaging exam ordered (e.g., MRI, CT, ultrasound, DEXA), the ordering location, scheduling preferences, and the appointment date and time once selected.
  • Message activity — the content of SMS messages sent to and received from the patient, delivery status (delivered, failed, undelivered), and timestamps.
  • Link interaction events — whether and when a patient opens a secure scheduling link, the device type, and the IP address from which the link was opened. We do not place advertising cookies or third-party trackers on our scheduling pages.
  • Customer-care records — notes captured by practice staff when a patient requests a callback or asks a question.

We deliberately minimize the amount of protected health information (PHI) that appears in any text message. SMS messages do not include diagnoses, payer details, implant questions, or other sensitive clinical detail.

2. How We Use Information

We use the information we collect solely to:

  • Send scheduling invitations, reminders, and confirmations for the patient’s ordered imaging exam.
  • Process replies (confirm, reschedule, callback request, HELP, STOP) and route exceptions to practice staff.
  • Generate calendar artifacts (such as .ics files) at the patient’s request.
  • Maintain an immutable audit trail of message events for operational, compliance, and patient-safety purposes.
  • Operate, secure, troubleshoot, and improve the Service.
  • Comply with applicable laws and respond to lawful requests.

3. We Do Not Sell or Share Your Data

We do not sell your information. We do not share or disclose your mobile phone number, identifiers, or message content with third parties for those third parties’ own marketing, advertising, or promotional purposes. We do not use your information for marketing of any kind.

Mobile information (including phone numbers and SMS opt-in status) is not shared with third parties or affiliates for marketing or promotional purposes. Information sharing with subprocessors described in Section 4 is limited to delivering the messaging and scheduling service the patient is using.

Personal information and SMS consent will not be shared with third parties or affiliates for marketing purposes.

4. Service Providers (Subprocessors)

We use a small number of vetted service providers strictly to operate the Program. These providers are contractually bound to use the information only to provide their services to us, to maintain appropriate security safeguards, and (where applicable) to comply with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) under a Business Associate Agreement. Categories include:

  • Telecommunications and SMS delivery (e.g., Twilio) — to transmit and receive text messages.
  • Cloud hosting and database providers — to host the Program’s servers and encrypted data.
  • Calendar and ICS generation libraries — to produce add-to-calendar artifacts.
  • The patient’s radiology practice and its electronic medical record (EMR) — the authoritative source of the patient’s medical record. ROCE is an orchestration layer; the EMR remains the system of record.

5. Legal Disclosures

We may disclose information when required by law, subpoena, court order, or other valid legal process, or where necessary to protect the rights, safety, or property of patients, the practice, or the public. Where disclosure is required by HIPAA or by other applicable law, we follow the standards set by those laws.

6. Data Retention

We retain message content, delivery events, and audit records for the period required to operate the Program, support the radiology practice’s recordkeeping obligations, and comply with applicable law. Information that is no longer needed for an operational or legal purpose is deleted or de-identified.

7. Security

We design ROCE as HIPAA-aware software. Security measures include, without limitation:

  • Encryption of data in transit (TLS) and encryption of sensitive data at rest where appropriate.
  • Use of opaque, signed, expiring tokens in patient-facing scheduling links instead of raw identifiers (no medical record number, order ID, appointment ID, or date of birth in URLs).
  • Role-based administrative access and least-privilege service accounts.
  • Audit logging of access to patient data.
  • Storage of vendor secrets in a dedicated secret manager rather than in source code or configuration files.

No security program is perfect, but we treat this Program as HIPAA-sensitive software from day one and continuously improve controls.

8. Your Choices

  • Opt out of SMS at any time by replying STOP to any Program message. See the Terms & Conditions for the full list of opt-out keywords.
  • Get help by replying HELP.
  • Update your phone number or preferences by contacting the radiology practice directly.
  • Request access to or correction of the medical record information held by the practice through the practice’s standard HIPAA request process.

9. Children’s Privacy

The Program is intended for adult patients of participating practices, or for minors whose parent or legal guardian is the consenting party. We do not knowingly enroll children under the age of 13 in SMS messaging without verifiable parental consent. If you believe a child has been enrolled inappropriately, please contact us using the information in Section 12.

10. International Users

The Program is operated from and intended for use within the United States. By participating, you understand that your information will be processed in the United States.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by an updated effective date at the top of this page and, where appropriate, by additional notice.
