About

What ROCE Does

The Radiology Order Conversion Engine (“ROCE”) is an internal operations platform used by radiology practices to help patients schedule, confirm, and complete imaging exams that have already been ordered by their physicians — including MRI, CT, ultrasound, and DEXA studies.

ROCE is not a replacement for an electronic medical record (EMR), a patient portal, or a marketing service. It is a focused tool that converts pre-authorized imaging orders into completed appointments through secure, low-PHI text messages, calendar invitations, and clinician-supervised exception handling.

SMS Program

The Patient Scheduling Text Program

Once a patient’s imaging order has been entered (and any required prior authorization has been approved), ROCE may send the patient one or more text messages to help them schedule and confirm the exam.

Message Types

  • An initial scheduling invitation containing a secure, expiring link.
  • Reminder messages 72 hours, 24 hours, and 3 hours before the appointment.
  • A confirmation request the day before the exam.
  • Replies to keywords HELP and STOP.

Message Frequency

Patients typically receive between 1 and 6 messages per imaging order, depending on how quickly they schedule and confirm.

Message and Data Rates

Message and data rates may apply, depending on the patient’s mobile plan. ROCE does not charge patients to participate.

Consent

How Patients Opt In

Patients only receive ROCE text messages after they have provided consent to be contacted by the radiology practice for scheduling purposes. Consent is collected through one or more of the following mechanisms:

  • At point of care — the patient provides a mobile phone number during registration with the radiology practice or referring office, and consents (in writing or verbally, recorded in the chart) to receive scheduling-related text messages from the practice.
  • At order entry — when an imaging order is placed, practice staff verbally confirm the patient agrees to receive scheduling text messages and document the consent in the patient’s medical record.
  • Web form / patient portal — the patient submits their phone number through a practice web form or patient portal page that includes an opt-in checkbox describing the SMS scheduling program and a link to these Terms and the Privacy Policy.
  • Reply-keyword opt-in — the patient texts the keyword START (or SCHEDULE, BEGIN, or YES) to the program number, after which an automated welcome message confirms enrollment.

Patients can opt out at any time by replying STOP, and can request help by replying HELP.
