About

What ROCE Does

The Radiology Order Conversion Engine (“ROCE”) is an internal operations platform used by radiology practices to help patients schedule, confirm, and complete imaging exams that have already been ordered by their physicians — including MRI, CT, ultrasound, and DEXA studies.

ROCE is not a replacement for an electronic medical record (EMR), a patient portal, or a marketing service. It is a focused tool that converts pre-authorized imaging orders into completed appointments through secure, low-PHI text messages, calendar invitations, and clinician-supervised exception handling.

SMS Program

The Patient Scheduling Text Program

Once a patient’s imaging order has been entered (and any required prior authorization has been approved), ROCE may send the patient one or more text messages to help them schedule and confirm the exam.

Message Types

  • An initial scheduling invitation containing a secure, expiring link.
  • Reminder messages 72 hours, 24 hours, and 3 hours before the appointment.
  • A confirmation request the day before the exam.
  • Replies to keywords HELP and STOP.

Message Frequency

Patients typically receive between 1 and 4 messages per appointment, depending on how quickly they schedule and confirm.

Message and Data Rates

Message and data rates may apply, depending on the patient’s mobile plan. ROCE does not charge patients to participate.

Consent

How Patients Opt In

Patients only receive ROCE text messages after they have provided consent to be contacted by the radiology practice for scheduling purposes. Consent is collected through a patient intake form before SMS messages are sent:

  • The patient provides or confirms their mobile phone number and checks an unchecked SMS consent box that says: I agree to receive scheduling-related text messages from Advanced Medical Imaging. Message & data rates may apply. Up to 4 messages per appointment. Reply STOP to opt out, HELP for help. See our Terms and Privacy Policy. Consent is not a condition of receiving medical care.
  • The intake form links to the Terms and Privacy Policy.
  • The intake form states that SMS consent is not a condition of receiving medical care.
  • Consent is stored with the patient ID, mobile phone number, timestamp, consent source, and Terms/Privacy version.

Patients can opt out at any time by replying STOP, and can request help by replying HELP.
