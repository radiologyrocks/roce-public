SMS Consent Forms and Staff Scripts

These examples document how patients consent to receive scheduling-related SMS messages before Advanced Medical Imaging sends appointment scheduling links, reminders, confirmations, or callback coordination messages.

Consent is for transactional healthcare scheduling only. Consent is not a condition of receiving medical care. Personal information and SMS consent will not be shared with third parties or affiliates for marketing purposes.

1) Point of Care Written Consent

The patient sees this consent language during in-person registration on paper or on a tablet. The checkbox starts unchecked and the patient signs or initials before SMS enrollment is recorded.

2) Verbal Consent Script

If consent is collected verbally, staff read the script below and document the patient's response in the medical record before any SMS messages are sent.

"We can send you text messages to help schedule your radiology appointment. Message & data rates may apply. Up to 4 messages per appointment. Reply STOP to opt out, HELP for help. SMS consent is not a condition of receiving medical care. Do you agree to receive these messages?"

3) Order Entry Staff Confirmation

When an imaging order is placed, staff confirm SMS consent using the verbal script above and record consent status before ROCE sends the first scheduling message.

  • Consent source: order-entry staff confirmation.
  • Stored fields: patient ID, mobile phone number, timestamp, staff user, consent source, and Terms/Privacy version.
  • Patients may opt out at any time by replying STOP and may request help by replying HELP.

The public example web form uses the same disclosures and is available for reviewer verification.
